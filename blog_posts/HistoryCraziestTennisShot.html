History's Craziest Tennis Shot
August 12, 2023

Tennis history is replete with astonishing shots that have left fans and fellow players in awe. While it's challenging to pinpoint a single "craziest" shot, one shot that stands out as both audacious and remarkable is Roger Federer's "Tweener" shot during the 2009 US Open.

In the fourth round of the tournament, Federer faced off against Novak Djokovic, his fierce rival and a formidable opponent. In the middle of a heated rally, Djokovic hit a deep lob over Federer, pushing him back to the baseline. With his back to the net and the ball seemingly out of reach, conventional wisdom suggested that Federer would reset the point by playing a safe shot.

However, what followed was pure magic. Instead of taking the safe option, Federer opted for an audacious "Tweener" shot. This is a shot played between the legs while facing away from the net, also known as a "between-the-legs" or "behind-the-back" shot. It is an extremely difficult shot to execute, requiring exceptional timing, reflexes, and confidence.

In this jaw-dropping moment, Federer managed to time the ball perfectly and send it back over the net with incredible accuracy and pace. Djokovic was caught off guard and could only watch in disbelief as the ball sailed over the net and landed for a clean winner on the other side.

The crowd erupted into cheers, and even Djokovic himself applauded the shot, recognizing the sheer brilliance and audacity of Federer's skill. The "Tweener" shot became an instant iconic moment in tennis history, showcasing Federer's extraordinary talent and ability to execute shots that defy imagination.

This shot epitomizes the essence of tennis and the extraordinary skills that the game's greatest players possess. While there have been numerous other incredible shots in tennis history, Federer's "Tweener" shot at the 2009 US Open remains etched in the memory of tennis fans as one of the craziest and most unforgettable shots ever played on a tennis court.
