Tennis: doubles
FEBRUARY 5, 2020

Hi! Today, we wanted to share the key differences between playing singles and doubles in tennis and some strategies to use when playing doubles. Essentially, the rules for doubles are the same as singles, except the court boundaries are wider. A tennis court has two lines on the left and right side running from the baseline to the net. Typically, in a game of singles, the horizontal boundaries are dictated by the second to last lines, but in doubles, the horizontal boundaries are moved to the farthest lines. In doubles, you and a partner play against a team of two players on the other side of the net. Additionally, the server rotates so that each player serves once every four games. Traditionally, the two players stand so each player takes one side of the court. Moreover, one player will stand at the baseline, covering the back of the court, while the other player covers the front half of the court.

A doubles team will typically be all-female or all-male. However, mixed doubles (where the teams consist of one female and one male) can also be played.

The strategies used in singles and doubles often differ as well. For example, in singles, a common tactic is to serve wide, causing the opponent off the court and then hitting the ball in the opposite direction into the open court. However, in doubles, this tactic is not as successful since the opponent's partner will be there to protect the open court. However, this tactic is still used since the opponent's partner will most likely be up at the net, so hitting the ball deep near the baseline will make it difficult for the player at the net to return it. Additionally, in doubles, players can play extreme angles without worry since they have the additional space of the doubles alleys.

A strategy that is unique to doubles tennis poaching. Many doubles teams play with both players at the net as often as possible because it is aggressive and can be very effective in a match. However, the majority of the time, one player is at the net while the other is at the baseline. In this formation, the player at the net can quickly slide into the center of the court and poach, or intercept, a shot intended for the player at the baseline. This technique is aggressive because it makes it difficult for the opponent to react to the net player's quick move and can help a team quickly rack up points.

We hope this read has been useful to you about some of the key differences between singles and doubles and some of the common strategies used by doubles players. Thanks for reading!
