Tennis to help with stress
JANUARY 22, 2020

If you are a high school or college student, this may be a very stressful month as finals are right around the corner. Even of you don't have finals, stress is something we all deal with, and it is well known that exercising can help decrease stress levels. Especially, playing tennis regularly is known to improve concentration and memory. This is because the sport requires both athletic skill, strategy, and collaboration, so it keeps you on your toes physically and mentally. Learning to keep your head in the game helps you stay more alert. As you hit the ball, tracking it with your mind and body, it feels as though time has slowed down, all of your worries clear your head, and your mind is completely focused on one task. This can be a very refreshing feeling. As your senses become more alert, your reactions and reflexes enter a sort of rhythm.

Tennis, as well as other forms of exercise, causes the brain to release endorphins and serotonin. Both of these are known to elevate your mood, along with other benefits. Regular tennis gives you a physical outlet for anxiety, depression, and daily worries. Playing regularly is great physical exercise and will result in a healthier body. This is extremely important in the short and long term. Maintaining your endurance and stamina is crucial for a long life. Your balance, coordination, speed, and flexibility will all improve. And, this enhanced physical state results in greater confidence in day to day life. As your game improves, your self-confidence will also continue to become stronger.

Regular tennis also gives you a regular physical outlet for stress. It gives you an opportunity to work out the muscle tension in your body associated with stress. It improves your cardiovascular health, which also helps your body better handle the daily stresses of life. Keep your body fit and strong, handle stress better, and improve your mental acuity. No other sport does it as well as tennis. Watching the pros play on TV, one thing is often apparent. These players are very, very close in skill level. Ultimately, it's their ability to handle pressure and turn that stress into extreme focus and determination that pushes them to the championship. Times when you are struggling to focus or when you just want to relax, tennis is one of the best ways to help. Tennis helps you feel better, think better, move better. And therefore, live better.
