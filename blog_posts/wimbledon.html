Wimbledon adapts to COVID
July 6, 2021

On Monday, June 28th, 2021, Wimbledon excitedly began its tournament after it was cancelled in 2020 due to the pandemic. It was yet again an eventful tournament that took place in the beautiful grass courts of London at the All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club. This year counted as the 134th year of Wimbledon.

To keep this tournament as safe as possible for spectators, they all had to test negative 48 hours prior to attending and or had to be fully vaccinated. However, not every match had a full crowd as some matches had no spectators.

However, some did have full capacity. Wimbledon consisted of men's singles and doubles, women's singles and doubles, mixed doubles, boys (under 18 – singles and doubles) and girls (under 18 – singles and doubles), which were also a part of the Grade A category of tournaments for under 18, and singles & doubles events for men's and women's wheelchair tennis players as part of the Uniqlo Tour under the Grand Slam category, also hosting singles and doubles events for wheelchair quad tennis for the first time. In the Men's Singles Title, Novak Djokovick successfully defended the title as he claimed his 20th Grand Slam tournament title. He defeated Matteo Berrettini in the final. For the Women's Single Title, it was won by Ashleigh Barty, who defeated Karolína Plíšková in the final.

This was a very monumental tournament in Wimbledon history as it marked the revival of grass tennis after the pandemic, and it was definitely one for the books. It is so nice to finally see tennis opening back up for the world again.
